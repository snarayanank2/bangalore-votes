## What is the GBA election?

Bengaluru's civic administration has been reorganised into the **Greater Bengaluru Authority (GBA)**, split into five corporations — North, South, East, West, and Central. This election chooses one **corporator** for each of the city's 369 wards under this new structure. It's the first ward-level election in the city in roughly a decade, so the wards, and often their boundaries and numbers, are new even to long-time residents.

## What does a corporator do?

A corporator is the elected representative for a single ward — the smallest unit of city government. Corporators:

- Represent their ward's residents in the corporation council.
- Raise and vote on local civic issues — roads, drainage, waste management, streetlights, parks, and similar ward-level concerns.
- Are usually a resident's first point of contact for civic grievances specific to their neighbourhood.

Unlike an MLA or MP, a corporator's mandate is hyper-local: your ward, not your assembly constituency or parliamentary constituency, which may have different boundaries entirely.

## Why does this election matter?

Ward-level decisions — which street gets fixed first, how waste is collected, where a park comes up — are shaped directly by who represents your ward. Because this is the first election under the new GBA structure, and the last ward election was so long ago, most voters are choosing a corporator for a ward that didn't exist in its current form the last time they voted. Knowing your ward and its candidates matters more than usual this time round.
